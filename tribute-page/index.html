Here's a time line of Sir CV Raman's life:

  • 1888 - Born in Tiruchinapalli, Madras (now Trichy, Tamil Nadu)
  • 1892 - Raman moved to Visakhapatnam with family
  • 1907 - Raman completed M.A. from University of Madras. He was posted in Calcutta (now Kolkata) as Assistant Accountant General
  • 1919 - Raman received two honorary positions at IACS. Honorary Professor and Honorary Secretary
  • 1924 - Raman was elected as Fellow of the Royal Society
  • 1926 - Raman established the Indian Journal of Physics
  • 1930 - Raman won the Nobel Prize in Physics "for his work on the scattering of light and for the discovery of the effect named after him
  • 1933 - Raman joined the Indian Institute of Science in Bangalore as its first Indian director
  • 1934 - Raman founded the Indian Academy of Sciences in 1934 and started publishing the proceedings of the academy
  • 1949 - Raman established the Raman Research Institute in Bangalore
  • 1954 - Raman was awarded the Bharat Ratna, the highest civilian award in India
  • 1957 - Raman was awarded the Lenin Peace Prize
  • 1970 - Raman died at the age of 82
